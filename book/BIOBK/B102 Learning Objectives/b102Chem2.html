BIO 102 Learning Objectives:

CHEMISTRY II: WATER AND ORGANIC MOLECULES

These learning objectives are taken from my Biology for Nonmajors class (BIO 102). I have tried to add a link to each that will direct you to a part of this chapter or another website that will facilitate your completion of the objective.

  1. Explain what is meant by the polarity of the water molecule, and how the polarity of water molecules allows them to interact with one another.
  2. Define hydrophilic and hydrophobic; relate these terms to different types of substances that contact water.
  3. Dissolved substances are called solutes; a fluid in which one or more substances can dissolve is called a solvent. Relate these concepts to a cup of coffee with two sugars.
  4. Define the terms acid and base, and give an example of each.
  5. The concentration of free hydrogen ions in solutions is measured by the pH scale. The pH of hair remover is 13; it is a base. The pH of vinegar is 3; it is an acid. What is the concentration of hydrogen ions in hair remover and vinegar, respectively?
  6. Organization of nearly all large biological molecules is influenced by their interactions with water; describe the interaction of water with a protein molecule.
  7. Name the three most abundant chemical elements in living things.
  8. List the four groups organic molecules.
  9. Define the term functional group and be able to recognize the major functional groups of organic compounds.
  10. Enzymes are a special class of proteins that speed up chemical reactions in cells. OLBB Chapter
  11. Condensation reactions result in the formation of covalent bonds between small molecules to form larger organic molecules. Condensation reactions remove water from their substrates. Describe what occurs during hydrolysis reactions.
  12. Define carbohydrates and list their functions.
  13. The simplest carbohydrates are sugar monomers, the monosaccharides; be able to give examples and their functions.
  14. A polysaccharide is a straight or branched chain of hundreds or thousands of sugar monomers, of the same or different kinds; be able to name common polysaccharides and state their function(s) in a cell.
  15. Define lipids and list their functions.
  16. Distinguish a saturated fat from an unsaturated fat.
  17. A phospholipid has two fatty acid tails attached to a glycerol backbone; cite the importance of these molecules to a cell.
  18. Define steroids and describe their chemical structure; cite the importance of the steroids known as cholesterol, and sex and other hormones.
  19. Describe proteins and list their functions and significance to a cell.
  20. Be able to sketch the three parts of every amino acid.
  21. Describe the complex structure of a protein through its primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary structure; relate this to the three-dimensional structure of proteins.
  22. Describe the three parts of every nucleotide; give examples of adenosine phosphates, nucleotide enzymes, and nucleic acids.
  23. Give the general functions of DNA and RNA molecules.
