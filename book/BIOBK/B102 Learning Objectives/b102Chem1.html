BIO 102 Learning Objectives:

CHEMISTRY I: ATOMS AND MOLECULES

These learning objectives are taken from my Biology for Nonmajors class (BIO 102). I have tried to add a link to each that will direct you to a part of this chapter or another website that will facilitate your completion of the objective.

  1. All forms of matter are composed of one or more elements. Explain how protons, electrons, and neutrons are arranged into atoms and ions.
  2. Define atomic number and atomic weight (or mass).
  3. Atoms with the same atomic number but a different mass number are isotopes. Lookup and list some of the isotopes of: Carbon, Oxygen, and Hydrogen.
  4. Define radioisotopes and list three ways they are useful.
  5. Describe the distribution of electrons in the space around the nucleus of an atom.
  6. An atom tends to react with other atoms when its outermost shell is only partly filled with electrons. This is the octet rule. How can the Periodic Table of the Elements be used to determine how many electrons are in the outer shell of an atom?
  7. Define the term ion and describe the conditions under which ionic bonds form between positive and negative ions.
  8. In a covalent bond, atoms share electrons. Distinguish between a nonpolar covalent bond and a polar covalent bond; give an example of each.
  9. Define hydrogen bond; describe conditions under which hydrogen bonds form and cite an example of a molecule that uses hydrogen bonds.
