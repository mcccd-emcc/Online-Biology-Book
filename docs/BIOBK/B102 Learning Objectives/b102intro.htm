BIO 102 Learning Objectives:

INTRODUCTION: THE NATURE OF SCIENCE AND BIOLOGY

These learning objectives are taken from my Biology for Nonmajors class (BIO 102). I have tried to add a link to each that will direct you to a part of this chapter or another website that will facilitate your completion of the objective.

  1. Name the special molecule (hint) that sets living things apart from the nonliving world; explain the functions of this molecule in general terms. OLBB Chapter
  2. A cell is the basic living unit. OLBB Chapter
  3. Distinguish between single-celled organisms and multicelled organisms. OLBB Chapter
  4. Arrange in order, from smallest to largest, the levels of organization that occur in nature. Define each as you list it.
  5. Metabolism means energy transfers within the cell. OLBB Chapter
  6. Contrast the general functions of the processes known as photosynthesis and aerobic respiration. OLBB Chapter OLBB Chapter
  7. Organisms use a molecule known as ATP to transfer chemical energy from one molecule to another. OLBB Chapter
  8. Homeostasis is defined as a state in which the conditions of an organism's internal environment are maintained within tolerable limits. OLBB Chapter
  9. Reproduction is the means by which each new organism arises. OLBB Chapter OLBB Chapter OLBB Chapter
  10. Explain the origin of trait variations that function in inheritance. OLBB Chapter
  11. A trait that assists an organism in survival and reproduction in a certain environment is said to be adaptive. OLBB Chapter
  12. As collections of organisms extend through time by successive generations, the character of populations changes; this is called evolution. OLBB Chapter
  13. Define natural selection and briefly describe what is occurring when a population is said to evolve. OLBB Chapter
  14. Explain the use of genus and species names by considering your Latin name, Homo sapiens. OLBB Chapter
  15. Arrange in order, from greater to fewer organisms included, the following categories of classification: class, family, genus, kingdom, order, phylum, and species. OLBB Chapter
  16. List the five kingdoms of life that are currently recognized by most scientists; tell generally what kinds of organisms are classified in each kingdom. OLBB Chapter
  17. Explain what is meant by the term biological diversity and speculate about what caused the great diversity of life forms on Earth. OLBB Chapter OLBB Chapter
  18. Outline a set of steps that might be used in the scientific method of investigating a problem.
  19. Explain why a control group is used in an experiment.
  20. Define what is meant by a theory; cite a biological example and one from another discipline of science.
