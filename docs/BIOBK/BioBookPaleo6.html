The Cenozoic era followed the mass extinction at the close of the Mesozoic era, 65 million years ago, and continues to the present. The Cenozoic is divided into two geological periods: the Tertiary period (65-1.8 million years ago), and the Quaternary (1.8 million years to the present). Because of the wealth of fossils and rocks of this most recent era, the se periods are divided into smaller subdivisions known as epochs. During the Cenozoic, the survivors of the mass extinction diversified into vacant ecological roles, and developed into new ones. The Cenozoic era is often referred to as the "age of mammals", although diversity of other groups also peaked during this era: birds, flowering plants, and modern teleost fish. A minor event occurred during the end of this era, the evolution of humans. However, this group of primates seems bent on causing a sixth mass extinction, which continues today.

The breakup of Pangaea continues, with modern-day rifting in east Africa. The collision of India with Asia built the Tibetan Plateau as well as the Himalayas. Climatic deterioration and global cooling over the past 40 million years has led to a long ice age during the past two million years.
